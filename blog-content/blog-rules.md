Helmful Content Writing Rules
Voice

Write like an experienced marine dealership consultant speaking to another industry professional.

Avoid sounding like a marketer, copywriter, or AI assistant.

Assume the reader runs a dealership and is short on time.

Use clear, direct language.

Prioritize practical advice over theory.

Sentence Structure

Mix short and medium-length sentences.

Do not make every paragraph the same size.

Occasionally use one-sentence paragraphs for emphasis.

Avoid overly polished transitions.

Avoid formulas such as:

"In today's fast-paced environment..."
"As the industry continues to evolve..."
"It's important to note..."
"In conclusion..."
"By leveraging..."
"Unlock the power of..."
Punctuation

Do not use em dashes (—).

Use commas or periods instead.

Do not overuse semicolons.

Do not use excessive bullet lists.

Avoid bolding every section.

Tone

Write with confidence.

Avoid hype.

Avoid exaggerated claims.

Do not describe everything as:

powerful
robust
innovative
seamless
game-changing
cutting-edge
revolutionary

Use specific examples instead.

Content Quality

Include real dealership scenarios.

Use numbers when possible.

Explain why something matters.

Provide actionable takeaways.

Every article should answer:

What is the problem?
Why does it happen?
What does it cost the dealership?
How can it be fixed?
Human Writing Patterns

Use occasional contractions:

don't
can't
won't
isn't

Vary sentence openings.

Avoid repeating the same phrase multiple times.

Avoid perfectly balanced lists of three.

Avoid ending every section with a summary sentence.

AI Phrases to Avoid

Never use:

delve into
landscape
navigate
unlock
elevate
empower
foster
leverage
streamline
optimize
transformative
comprehensive guide
ever-changing
fast-paced
digital age
in today's world
SEO

Write for dealership operators first.

Write for search engines second.

Do not force keywords into every heading.

Use natural language.

Prioritize readability over keyword density.

Examples

Bad:

"By leveraging a comprehensive CRM solution, marine dealerships can streamline operations and unlock new growth opportunities."

Good:

"Most dealerships lose track of leads because sales activity lives in spreadsheets, inboxes, and text messages. A CRM puts that information in one place."

Bad:

"In today's competitive marine landscape, dealerships must embrace digital transformation."

Good:

"Boat buyers expect a response within hours, not days. Many dealerships still struggle to meet that expectation."
